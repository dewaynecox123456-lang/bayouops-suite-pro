# BayouOps Suite Pro Video Shot List

## Recording Style

Use a clean screen recording with steady cursor movement, moderate zoom-ins, and no rapid scrolling. The pacing should feel like an operational walkthrough, not a product teaser. Keep transitions simple: cut, short crossfade, or direct screen change.

## Scene Sequence

## 1. Opening Title - 0:00-0:05

**Shot:** BayouOps logo, dashboard, or clean project screen.  
**Lower-third/title:** "BayouOps Suite Pro - Operational Readiness & Visibility"  
**Pacing:** Hold long enough for the title to be readable.

## 2. Operational Problem - 0:05-0:30

**Shot:** Show sample folders, exports, spreadsheets, or report files that represent fragmented operational evidence.  
**Camera/screen guidance:** Slowly pan across files or use a controlled cursor path.  
**Zoom-in:** Highlight multiple evidence sources such as patch readiness, risk summary, stale systems, and SSL findings.  
**Transition:** Cut to dashboard.

## 3. Dashboard First Look - 0:30-0:55

**Shot:** Main dashboard or operational summary.  
**Lower-third/title:** "One readiness view for patch and exposure review"  
**Zoom-in:** Readiness totals, risk categories, and red/yellow/green indicators.  
**Pacing:** Let each major area remain visible for at least two seconds.

## 4. Patch Window Pressure - 0:55-1:15

**Shot:** Readiness details or patch export.  
**Screen guidance:** Show the status items that would matter before a change window.  
**Zoom-in:** Systems needing review, missing evidence, or readiness blockers.  
**Transition:** Short crossfade or direct cut to risk scoring.

## 5. Readiness and Risk Scoring - 1:15-1:45

**Shot:** Risk score, readiness score, or categorized findings.  
**Lower-third/title:** "Technical drift translated into operational status"  
**Zoom-in:** One critical or high-risk item.  
**Pacing:** Pause after highlighting the item so the narration can explain why it matters.

## 6. Stale Systems - 1:45-2:05

**Shot:** Stale-system list or export.  
**Screen guidance:** Keep hostnames sanitized if needed.  
**Zoom-in:** Last-seen date, status, owner, or review field.  
**Transition:** Cut to SSL/certificate view.

## 7. SSL and Certificate Findings - 2:05-2:25

**Shot:** SSL or certificate findings summary.  
**Lower-third/title:** "Certificate risk belongs in readiness reporting"  
**Zoom-in:** Expired or expiring certificate example.  
**Pacing:** Keep the finding visible while explaining operational impact.

## 8. Reporting Workflow - 2:25-2:45

**Shot:** Exports folder, report list, CSV, and executive summary.  
**Screen guidance:** Open one technical export and one management-ready summary.  
**Zoom-in:** File names, timestamps, or summary status lines.  
**Transition:** Return to dashboard.

## 9. CAB, SOX, and Executive View - 2:45-3:05

**Shot:** Executive summary or management-ready output.  
**Lower-third/title:** "Evidence for CAB, SOX, and management updates"  
**Zoom-in:** Plain-language summary or exception list.  
**Pacing:** Use a slightly slower cursor movement here because this is the business-value section.

## 10. Closing Value - 3:05-3:20

**Shot:** Dashboard with BayouOps branding visible.  
**Lower-third/title:** "Answer: Are we exposed?"  
**Screen guidance:** End on the consolidated readiness view.  
**Transition:** Fade out or hold final frame.

## Suggested 10-Minute Additions

- Add a short setup scene explaining the current patch cycle.
- Spend additional time on stale-system validation.
- Open and explain each export type.
- Show how the same evidence supports admin review, CAB review, and executive reporting.
- Include before-and-after views of scattered files versus consolidated dashboard.

## Lower-Third Suggestions

- "Operational Readiness & Visibility"
- "Patch Window Pressure"
- "Stale Systems Need Review"
- "SSL and Certificate Risk"
- "CAB and SOX Evidence"
- "Executive-Ready Exposure Reporting"

## Pacing Notes

- Keep the 3-minute version tight and focused.
- Avoid more than one major idea per scene.
- Use zoom-ins only when the viewer needs to read a specific finding.
- Do not over-animate transitions.
- End on a practical operational statement rather than a marketing slogan.
